You have seen the posts: "Comment GUIDE and I'll DM you the link." The creator types one caption, and a tool delivers the link to everyone who comments, automatically. That is comment-to-DM automation, and setting it up takes about 15 minutes once your account is ready. Here is exactly how to do it, the Instagram rules you have to follow, and what it costs.

## What is Instagram comment-to-DM automation?

Instagram comment-to-DM automation sends an automatic direct message to anyone who comments a chosen keyword on your post, using the official Instagram API, available to Business and Creator accounts. Someone comments your keyword, the tool detects it, and a DM lands in their inbox with whatever you set up: a link, a discount code, a PDF, or a question that starts a conversation.

It is the highest-intent funnel on Instagram because the person raised their hand first. They commented, so the DM is welcome rather than cold. Done right, it turns a single reel into a list-building machine.

## What you need before you start

Three things, and the first two are free:

- **An [Instagram Business or Creator account](https://help.instagram.com/).** Comment-to-DM runs on Meta's official Instagram API, which is only available to Business and Creator accounts, not personal ones. Switching is free and takes two minutes in your profile settings.
- **A connected Facebook Page.** The Instagram API authorizes through a linked Facebook Page. You do not have to post to the Page; it just satisfies Meta's connection requirement.
- **An official-API comment-to-DM tool.** This is the one paid piece. The tool must use the official Instagram API (Meta-approved), not a password login. [Botyoo](https://botyoo.com/) does this from $3.60/mo flat-rate; other options exist at higher prices.

Avoid any tool that asks for your Instagram username and password. That is scraping, it violates Meta's terms, and it is the fastest way to get your account restricted.

## How to set up Instagram comment-to-DM automation

Setting up Instagram comment-to-DM automation takes seven steps and about 15 minutes: switch to a Business account, connect a tool, pick the post and keyword, write the DM, add an email step, test it live, then monitor. Here is each step.

1. **Switch to a Business or Creator account.** In Instagram, go to Settings, then Account type, and switch. Connect it to a Facebook Page when prompted.
2. **Connect your account to an official-API tool.** Sign up for your tool and authorize Instagram through Meta's official login screen. You are granting the app permission through Meta, never handing over your password.
3. **Create a comment-to-DM trigger.** Choose which post the automation watches (a specific reel, or all future posts) and set the keyword that fires it, like `LINK`, `GUIDE`, or `PRICE`.
4. **Write the DM.** Compose the message that gets sent. Keep it short, lead with the thing they asked for, and add one clear call to action. Set the public comment reply too (for example, "Sent! Check your DMs.") so commenters know it worked.
5. **Add an email-capture step (optional but recommended).** If your tool supports it, ask for an email inside the DM flow before you hand over the link. This turns a comment into a subscriber. Botyoo captures the email natively and syncs it to your list; most cheaper tools cannot.
6. **Test it on a live post.** Comment your own keyword from a second account and confirm the DM arrives. Always test before you promote the post.
7. **Monitor and optimize.** Watch which keywords and posts convert, then reuse the winners. Your analytics dashboard shows opens and click-throughs per flow.

That is the whole setup. The first one takes 15 minutes; every one after that takes about three.

## Instagram's comment-to-DM rules you need to know

Comment-to-DM is allowed because it responds to a user-initiated action, but Instagram's API enforces hard limits you cannot exceed. Knowing them keeps your account safe.

- **One private reply per comment.** You can send one automated DM to a person per comment they leave. You cannot blast a commenter repeatedly.
- **A 7-day response window.** You have up to 7 days to send the automated DM after the comment (for Instagram Live, you can only message during the broadcast).
- **200 automated DMs per hour, per account.** Instagram's Graph API caps automated messages at roughly 200 per hour. Past that, automation pauses for about an hour, then resumes. A viral post still gets everyone, just spread across the queue.
- **User-initiated only.** Meta only permits automated DMs in response to a user action, like a comment, story reply, or incoming DM. You cannot cold-DM people who never interacted. These limits are documented in [Meta's Instagram Platform docs](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/instagram-platform/).

None of these are tool-specific; they are Instagram's rules, identical across every official-API tool. Any tool promising unlimited cold DMs is breaking them.

## Comment-to-DM best practices

A few habits separate funnels that convert from ones that get ignored:

- **Put the keyword in the caption and say it out loud.** "Comment GUIDE for the free checklist" outperforms hoping people guess. Tell them exactly what to type.
- **Pick a simple, single keyword.** One short word beats a phrase. `GUIDE` is better than `send me the guide please`.
- **Deliver value first, pitch second.** Lead the DM with the thing they asked for. Earn the click before you ask for anything.
- **Capture the email before the link.** A link is a one-time visit; an email is a relationship. Ask for it in the flow.
- **Don't fake it.** Only automate replies to real triggers. Buying comment pods or spamming breaks Instagram's rules and your credibility.
