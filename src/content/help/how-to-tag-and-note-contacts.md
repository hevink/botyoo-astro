# How to tag and note contacts

Tags and notes are the lightweight way to track context on individual contacts, without building a folder for every category.

## Step 1: Open the contact

Click any conversation in the Inbox.

## Step 2: Find the contact side-panel

The right side of the open conversation shows the contact's profile. Scroll to **Tags** and **Notes**.

![Contact side-panel with Tags and Notes sections](/help-screenshots/inbox-side-panel-tags-notes.png)

## Adding a tag

Click the **+** next to Tags. Type a tag name and press Enter. Existing tags appear in a dropdown for quick reuse.

![Tag creation input with autocomplete](/help-screenshots/inbox-tag-create.png)

Example tags:

- `interested-in-courses`
- `asked-about-pricing`
- `paid-customer`
- `replied-to-flash-sale`

## Adding a note

Click into the **Write a note…** field. Type. Notes are private to your workspace, the contact never sees them.

![Note field with a note being typed](/help-screenshots/inbox-note-create.png)

Example notes:

- "Asked about bulk discount, said budget is $500"
- "Buys every quarter, invite to early access"
- "Don't follow up, opted out by DM"

## Using tags in campaigns

Tags feed into campaign audience targeting. Create a campaign, pick **By tag**, and select your tag. Every contact with that tag becomes part of the audience.

![Campaign audience picker with tag selected](/help-screenshots/campaign-audience-by-tag.png)

## Tags vs folders

- **Tags**, inline labels for a single contact based on a conversation
- **Folders**, reusable buckets you sort many contacts into at scale

Use tags for one-off context, folders for systematic segmentation.
